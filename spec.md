Kintsugi inspired mural showcasing human emotion

A living, crowd-sourced art installation where human vulnerability becomes collective beauty.
Students submit responses for a proudest moment, a biggest regret, or a half-finished dream. Each response is transformed by AI into a unique artwork, displayed as a jagged shard in an ever-growing mosaic. Their own words float over the image they inspired. Every shard connects to others through thick gold threads — a digital Kintsugi, where the cracks between stories become the most beautiful part.

The mural fills the screen like r/place, dense and alive. Zoom in and the world expands infinitely — each shard reveals more detail, more words, more of someone's interior life. The closer you look, the more human it becomes.
Inspired by the Japanese philosophy of Kintsugi — the art of repairing broken pottery with gold, treating fracture not as damage but as history worth honoring.
The more people contribute, the more whole it becomes.

Workflow:
Have students respond to a Google form where they can choose to respond to any of the three questions (proudest moment, biggest regret, half finished project)
Responses get stored into a google spreadsheet
Responses get parsed and a prompt is generated for each prompt to turn it into an image
All the images are displayed as shards in a mural and connections are bound together by gold threads
The words that they wrote in the form will be displayed in the shard over the image
Using a quadtree structure you can zoom into each image infinitely and explore people’s emotions. 
If you click the combine button → Create a 3D piece of pottery using kintsugi with all the pictures as the shards. You can drag around in space and see all the pictures together combined by gold. Still have the option to go into the image.
